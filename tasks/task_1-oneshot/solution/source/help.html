How to use this chat

Welcome! If you've never used a chat tool before, don't worry — this page walks you through every part of it in plain words. You type a question, a real person at City Hall types back, and you can take your time. There's nothing to download, no account to make, and no wrong way to ask.

Common questions, answered simply

Tap a question to read the answer. Start with whichever one is on your mind.

What is a chat?

A chat is a back-and-forth conversation that you have by typing. You write a short message (a sentence or two is fine), press send, and someone writes back to you. It's a lot like texting a friend — except you're texting your city. You can leave and come back later; your conversation will still be here.

Is this free?

Yes. Chatting with City Hall is completely free. There is no charge for asking a question, reporting a pothole, or getting help with a permit. If a city service has a fee (like a building permit), we will always tell you the cost up front before anything is charged.

Who am I talking to?

A real person who works for the City of Riverbend. Every helper introduces themselves by name and which department they're in (for example, "Hi, I'm Marcus from Public Works"). On weekdays from 8am to 6pm, the wait is usually under three minutes.

Will my message be private?

Yes. Only you and the city staff member helping you can read the chat. We don't share your conversations with anyone outside City Hall, and we never sell your information. You can read the full privacy promise on our privacy page.

What if I don't know what to ask?

That's okay — you don't have to use special words. Just describe what's happening in your own way. "The garbage truck didn't come" or "there's a hole in the road by my house" are perfect ways to start. The helper will ask follow-up questions if they need more details.

What if I make a mistake or change my mind?

No problem at all. You can correct yourself in the next message — just type "actually, I meant…" and keep going. If you want to stop the chat, you can simply close the window. Nothing is final until you and the helper agree on what to do next.

Prefer not to chat? Three other ways to reach us

Chat is just one option. Pick whichever feels most comfortable.

Call us on the phone

(555) 348-2100 · Monday – Friday, 8:00 am to 6:00 pm. Saturdays 9:00 am to 1:00 pm. Closed Sundays and city holidays. A real person picks up — no automated menu.

Walk in to City Hall

200 Riverbend Plaza, Suite 110 · Open Monday – Thursday, 8:30 am to 5:00 pm and Fridays 8:30 am to 12:00 pm. Free parking in the lot behind the building. No appointment needed for general questions.

Send a paper form by mail

Resident Services, City of Riverbend, P.O. Box 4408, Riverbend, OR 97042 · Printable forms are available at the front desk or at your branch library. We reply by mail within 7 business days.

  • Still stuck? There is no wrong way to ask. If you tell us what you're trying to do in your own words, we'll figure out the rest together.
  • Need someone to help in person? Visit any branch library on Wednesday afternoons — librarians can sit with you and walk through the chat step by step.
